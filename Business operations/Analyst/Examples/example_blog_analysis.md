## SECTION 4: GEO/AI CITATION ANALYSIS

### Citation-Worthy Sentences

1. **Sentence:** "Channel letter signs are three-dimensional illuminated letters or logos fabricated from aluminum returns, acrylic faces, and internal LED lighting modules."
   - **Type:** Definition
   - **Why quotable:** Clear, complete definition that stands alone; technical but accessible; AI systems love standalone definitions

2. **Sentence:** "LED channel letters consume approximately 1-2 watts per linear foot, which is 60-70% less energy than traditional neon signage."
   - **Type:** Statistic / Comparison
   - **Why quotable:** Specific numerical data; comparative statement; energy efficiency is common query; verifiable claim

3. **Sentence:** "UL-listed LED modules in channel letters have a rated lifespan of 50,000 hours, equivalent to approximately 11 years of operation at 12 hours per day."
   - **Type:** Statistic / Technical specification
   - **Why quotable:** Industry standard referenced (UL); specific timeframe; real-world context provided (hours to years conversion)

4. **Sentence:** "The installation process for channel letter signs typically requires 4-8 hours for a standard 10-letter set, depending on mounting surface and electrical access."
   - **Type:** Process / Timeframe
   - **Why quotable:** Specific timeframe data; provides context (letter count); addresses common "how long" query

5. **Sentence:** "Front-lit channel letters feature translucent acrylic faces that illuminate when internal LEDs are powered, creating bright, eye-catching signage visible from over 500 feet in optimal conditions."
   - **Type:** Definition / Technical specification
   - **Why quotable:** Explains how it works; includes visibility metric; technical + practical info combined

### GEO Gaps

**Missing definitions:**
- No clear definition of "returns" in sign terminology
- "Raceway" mentioned but never explained
- UL listing explained but NEC compliance not defined

**Vague claims that need data:**
- "Very durable" → should specify wind load rating, impact resistance
- "Low maintenance" → should quantify maintenance frequency/costs
- "Cost-effective" → should provide specific ROI timeline or comparison

**Poor structure (need tables/lists):**
- Cost factors explained in paragraphs → would be clearer as table
- Types of channel letters → comparison table would help
- Material options → specifications table missing

**Questions not answered:**
- "What permits are required?" (mentioned but not detailed)
- "Can channel letters be repaired?" (maintenance mentioned but not repair)
- "What's the difference between aluminum and stainless steel returns?" (not covered)
