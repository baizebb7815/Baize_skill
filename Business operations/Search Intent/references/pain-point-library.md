## Using This Library

### How to Apply Pain Points

1. **Start with buyer type**: B2B or B2C?
2. **Select relevant category**: Installation, Financial, Quality, etc.
3. **Identify specific pain point**: Find the closest match to your product/scenario
4. **Use the fear statement**: This is what the buyer is thinking
5. **Apply search pattern**: Natural language they'd use in Google
6. **Determine reassurance needed**: What would ease their concern
7. **Select content type**: What format best addresses this

### Example Application

**Scenario**: B2B coffee shop owner considering LED neon sign

**Selected pain points**:
1. Electrician dependency (Installation category)
2. Total cost uncertainty (Financial category)
3. Permit uncertainty (Compliance category)

**Keywords generated**:
- "do led neon signs need electrician to install"
- "total cost of neon sign for coffee shop"
- "do i need permit for storefront neon sign"

**Content angles**:
- "DIY vs Electrician for Coffee Shop Neon Signs: Cost & Difficulty Guide"
- "True Cost Calculator: Neon Signs for Coffee Shops (Product + Install + Permits)"
- "Storefront Neon Sign Permits: State-by-State Requirements & Timelines"

### Customizing for New Industries

When researching new industries, create new pain points by:

1. **Interview/research**: What keeps buyers up at night?
2. **Forum mining**: Reddit, Quora, industry forums
3. **Review analysis**: What complaints appear in product reviews?
4. **Sales call transcripts**: What objections come up repeatedly?
5. **Support tickets**: What pre-purchase questions are common?

Add new pain points to this library using the same structure:
- Clear pain point name
- Fear statement (buyer's inner monologue)
- Search patterns (how they'd Google this)
- Reassurance needed (what calms their concern)
- Content type (best format to address it)
