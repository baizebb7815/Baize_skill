## üë§ POV & Character System (MANDATORY)

### Default Perspective: First-Person POV

**Why POV?**
- Maximum realism
- High AI consistency
- Zero face generation errors
- Scalable & repeatable

### What to Show in Frame
‚úÖ **Always include:**
- Hands performing actions
- Tools being used
- Products being handled
- Materials being worked with
- Installation process

‚ùå **Never include:**
- Human faces
- Full body shots
- Reflections showing faces
- People in background

### POV Shot Examples

**Installation POV:**
```
Camera angle: Slightly tilted down, as if looking at your own hands
Frame: Hands holding drill, LED sign, wall in background
Action: Installing mounting bracket
```

**Unboxing POV:**
```
Camera angle: Looking down into box
Frame: Hands opening packaging, product revealed
Action: Lifting sign out of protective foam
```

**Workshop POV:**
```
Camera angle: Over-the-shoulder perspective
Frame: Workbench with tools, hands assembling components
Action: Connecting wires, testing LED module
```

## üìê Composition Guidelines

### Framing Principles

**The "Triangle of Trust":**
1. **Clear subject** - Sign/product/hands always identifiable
2. **Context** - Enough background to feel real
3. **Action** - Movement/progress visible

### Shot Composition Rules

**Close-ups (60% of shots):**
- Hands + product filling 70% of frame
- Shallow depth acceptable
- Focus on detail/texture

**Medium shots (30% of shots):**
- Show context (wall, room, workspace)
- Subject still prominent (50% of frame)
- Reveal installation environment

**Wide shots (10% of shots - use sparingly):**
- Only for before/after comparisons
- Establishing context
- Final reveal shots

---

## üé¨ Camera Movement Standards

### Allowed Movements

**Static (70% of shots):**
- Locked camera, subject moves
- Ideal for: Close-ups, detail work, text overlays

**Push-in (20% of shots):**
- Slow zoom toward subject
- Speed: 2-3 seconds to complete
- Use for: Emphasizing detail, creating focus

**Pull-back (10% of shots):**
- Reveal context/environment
- Speed: 3-4 seconds to complete
- Use for: Before/after, final reveals

### Movement Speed Reference

```
Slow: Barely perceptible (trust-building pace)
Medium: Noticeable but smooth (demonstration pace)
Fast: AVOID (breaks realism)
```
